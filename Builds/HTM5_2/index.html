The Orbital Stability of an orbit is defined by how long how long an object in the orbit is able to maintain the same predictable path. For example a planet orbiting a star may be able to maintain its orbit for billions of years making the orbit very stable. On the other hand a satellite placed between the sun and the earth may only take sever weeks before corrections must be made to maintain the orbit.

The Stability of orbits is an important factor in deciding where satellites are placed as well as determining what we can expect to happen to objects in space over a long period of time.

Information about the simulation:
-All objects are to scale when zoomed in (if they would be too small to see on screen the visible size is increased as needed)
-Physics Calculations take place 21600 times a second (i.e. once every 4 simulated seconds when running at 1 day/s, and once every 100 seconds when running at 25 days/s)
-If the simulation isn't able to do all the calculations it needs, the time real-time clock will slow down
-Running at higher speeds will make the simulation less accurate (shouldn't really be a problem) but will not be more intensive on the computer because the number of physics steps per second is constant.
-All position velocity, radius information are calculated using double precision numbers (15 significant digits, with exponent component ranging 10 to the +/- 308 power
-Due to the nature of how the calculations are done they are not perfectly accurate and will accumulate error over time, especially if running at higher speeds.
-All calculations take place in 3D but i placed everything in 2D because its makes it easier to see and because the visuals can only display in 2D
-All physics engine and gravity code was writen by me (Pushpinder)
